Volunteer Handbook

Program Areas

We offer volunteer opportunities in the following programs:

  • Food Distribution: Help sort, pack, and distribute food at our community pantries. Shifts available Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday mornings.
  • Youth Mentoring: Mentor young people ages 12-18 in academic and life skills. Requires a background check and 6-month minimum commitment. Training provided.
  • Disaster Relief: Assist with emergency response efforts including shelter setup, supply distribution, and community outreach. On-call basis with advance notice when possible.
  • Administrative Support: Help with data entry, phone calls, event planning, and office tasks. Flexible scheduling available.

How to Sign Up

  1. Call our volunteer line or use the chatbot to express interest.
  2. A volunteer coordinator will contact you within 24 hours.
  3. Complete the volunteer application and orientation (approximately 2 hours).
  4. For Youth Mentoring, complete the background check process (5-7 business days).
  5. Begin volunteering at your scheduled time.

Requirements

  • Minimum age: 16 years (18 for Youth Mentoring and Disaster Relief).
  • All volunteers must complete a 2-hour orientation session.
  • Background checks required for programs involving minors or vulnerable populations.
  • Volunteers must commit to a minimum of 4 hours per month.

Scheduling

Volunteer shifts are typically 3-4 hours. You can sign up for shifts through our volunteer portal or by calling the volunteer coordinator. We ask for at least 48 hours notice if you need to cancel a shift.

Recognition

We value our volunteers and recognize contributions through:

  • Annual volunteer appreciation event
  • Service hour certificates for students
  • Milestone awards at 100, 500, and 1000 hours
  • Letters of recommendation upon request
