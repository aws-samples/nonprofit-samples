Crisis Support Resources

Emergency Contacts

  • Life-threatening emergencies: Call 911
  • Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
  • SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-4357

Our Crisis Helpline

Our trained specialists are available to provide support, referrals, and resources. Our helpline operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

When you call our crisis helpline:

  1. You will be connected to a specialist as quickly as possible (typical wait time under 2 minutes).
  2. All calls are confidential and may be recorded for quality assurance.
  3. Our specialists can provide emotional support, safety planning, and referrals to local services.
  4. If you call outside business hours, you can leave a callback number and a specialist will return your call within 30 minutes.

What to Expect

Our crisis specialists are trained to:

  • Listen without judgment
  • Help you assess your immediate safety
  • Create a safety plan if needed
  • Connect you with local resources and services
  • Follow up within 24-48 hours if requested

Community Resources

We maintain partnerships with local organizations that provide:

  • Emergency shelter and housing assistance
  • Food assistance and meal programs
  • Mental health counseling (sliding scale fees available)
  • Legal aid and advocacy services
  • Job training and employment assistance
  • Substance abuse treatment referrals

For Supervisors and Agents

Crisis calls are automatically flagged as priority 1 in the system. Supervisors can monitor these calls in real-time and join the call if needed using the barge-in feature. All crisis calls should be documented in the contact notes with a follow-up action if applicable.
